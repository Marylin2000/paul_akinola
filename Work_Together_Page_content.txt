Work Together Page Implementation Spec
Page goal
Turn clarity into conversation without pressure.
Primary outcome
The visitor feels seen, understands how the conversation starts, and knows where to respond.
Page character
Calm, thoughtful, low-pressure, and clear.
1. Sitemap reference and page position
This page must directly represent the Work Together branch of the sitemap. Nothing should be implied. Each sitemap section should appear as a visible page block or as a clearly defined interaction element.
Sitemap structure: Opening → Who this is for → What we do → How it starts → Context (Work / Life) → CTA → Return paths
User flow for this page: Recognition → Relevance → Trust → Low-pressure process → Context selection → Conversation or continued exploration
Role in the wider site: This page is where recognition from Work, Inner Life, Thoughts, or About becomes a clear next step. It should not feel like a sales page. It should feel like the place where understanding becomes conversation.
2. Global page build rules
• Header navigation: Home | Work | Inner Life | Thoughts | About | Work Together. 'Work Together' should appear as the active item.
• Tone: Clear, grounded, personal, and calm. No hype, no consultant-style claims, and no pressure language.
• Layout character: Text-led, spacious, and restrained. Use typography, spacing, and very light tonal shifts before using heavy boxes or decorative elements.
• Content width: Reading sections should stay around 680 to 760 px max. Wider bands can extend to 1200 px max with strong side padding.
• Responsive behaviour: Any two-column section stacks cleanly on tablet and mobile. Recognition lists and return-path cards should preserve spacing when stacked.
• Interactions: Buttons, cards, and form states should be subtle. No aggressive animation, hard shadows, or loud hover treatments.
• Conversion style: The page must lower resistance. It should make conversation feel simple, safe, and useful.
3. Section-by-section implementation
Section 1. Opening
Role: Welcome the visitor into conversation without sounding transactional.
Layout: Clean hero section. Full width. Headline and support copy in a restrained column. Optional faint structural line pattern in the background, but keep it very light.
Build note: This section should feel warm and confident. It should read like an invitation, not a pitch.
Final copy: 
Eyebrow: Work Together
Headline: If this feels familiar, this is a good place to start.
Support copy: Sometimes what is missing is not more effort. It is a clearer understanding of what is actually shaping the outcome. This page is where that understanding turns into conversation.
Section 2. Who this is for
Role: Help the visitor recognise themselves quickly. This is the key relevance block on the page.
Layout: Section heading, short intro, then a recognition list. Desktop can use a two-column list. Mobile should stack to one column. Keep the list readable and not overly boxed in.
Build note: The list should feel like recognition, not qualification. Avoid making it sound exclusive or application-based.
Final copy: 
Section heading: This may be for you if any of these feel familiar.
Section intro: You do not need to arrive with the full language for it. Sometimes it is enough to know that something is not adding up.
Recognition points:
• Your team is active, but the results still feel weaker than they should.
• Decisions are being made, but not from a place of full clarity.
• Revenue, pipeline, growth, or execution feels noisy rather than trustworthy.
• You know something deeper is shaping the outcome, but you have not been able to name it clearly.
• You feel stuck in a pattern that does not make sense on the surface.
• You are trying to move forward, but something within you keeps interrupting the process.
• You want to understand what is really going on before jumping into more action.
• You are looking for a calmer, clearer way to think about what comes next.
Section 3. What we do
Role: Explain the nature of the work without turning the page into a services menu.
Layout: Short intro copy followed by four simple principle blocks or a four-step horizontal / vertical sequence.
Build note: This section should communicate how the conversation works. Keep the language human and exact. Avoid packaged-offer language.
Final copy: 
Section heading: We start by understanding what is actually going on.
Intro: I do not start by forcing a framework over your situation. I start by listening carefully, looking for what sits underneath the surface, and helping you see the structure more clearly.
Principle 1 title: Name what is visible
Principle 1 body: We begin with what is not adding up on the surface.
Principle 2 title: Look underneath it
Principle 2 body: We look for the systems, patterns, pressures, or hidden assumptions shaping the outcome.
Principle 3 title: Clarify what matters
Principle 3 body: We separate signal from noise so the real issue becomes easier to see.
Principle 4 title: Decide the next move
Principle 4 body: Once there is clarity, it becomes easier to know what to address, what to change, and what not to force.
Section 4. How it starts
Role: Reduce friction by making the first step simple and concrete.
Layout: Three-step sequence or three compact cards. Keep visual treatment lighter than a SaaS process diagram.
Build note: The visitor should feel that this is easy to begin. Simplicity matters more than sophistication here.
Final copy: 
Section heading: How it starts
Intro: Simple. Start with a short note about what is not adding up.
Step 1 title: Share what feels unclear
Step 1 body: Tell me, in plain language, what feels off, stuck, noisy, or hard to name.
Step 2 title: We have a first conversation
Step 2 body: We look at what is happening on the surface and what may be shaping it underneath.
Step 3 title: We decide what makes sense
Step 3 body: If there is useful next work, we define it clearly. If not, you still leave with better language and direction.
Section 5. Context
Role: Show that the page holds both sides of the site: work and inner life.
Layout: Two equal cards side by side on desktop, stacked on mobile. Both cards should feel equally valid. Do not visually subordinate one to the other.
Build note: This section matters because the whole site holds these two paths together. It should feel integrated, not split between business and personal branding.
Final copy: 
Section heading: Where this usually shows up
Work card title: In work
Work card body: This may show up in performance, growth, revenue, CRM structure, team alignment, leadership decisions, or the gap between activity and real outcomes.
Work card CTA label: See Systems at Work
Work card CTA destination: /work
Life card title: In life
Life card body: This may show up in clarity, identity, internal conflict, repeated patterns, direction, faith, hope, or the sense that something deeper is shaping how you respond.
Life card CTA label: See Systems in Life
Life card CTA destination: /inner-life
Section 6. Primary CTA
Role: Create the clearest conversion point on the page.
Layout: A quiet full-width CTA band followed immediately by the contact method. This can be a compact form block, an email CTA, or both. Keep it minimal.
Build note: The main CTA must feel like the natural next step, not a demand. This is the most important section on the page.
Final copy: 
Section heading: Start a conversation.
Body copy: If you are trying to make sense of what is not adding up, send a short note. Start where you are. We can work out the rest from there.
Primary CTA label: Start a Conversation
Section 7. Return paths
Role: Give visitors a softer next step if they are not ready to reach out yet.
Layout: Three lighter cards or text links. Desktop can use a three-column layout. Mobile should stack cleanly.
Build note: These are genuine alternatives, not distractions. The page should still feel generous even when someone is not ready to convert.
Final copy: 
Section heading: Or keep exploring first.
Thoughts title: Notes and reflections
Thoughts body: Read the ideas behind the site and follow the thinking more deeply.
Thoughts CTA label: Go to Thoughts
Thoughts CTA destination: /thoughts
Work title: Systems at Work
Work body: Explore how systems shape performance, decisions, revenue, and growth.
Work CTA label: Go to Work
Work CTA destination: /work
Life title: Systems in Life
Life body: Explore how inner systems shape behaviour, identity, resilience, faith, and meaning.
Life CTA label: Go to Inner Life
Life CTA destination: /inner-life
Section 8. Footer transition
Role: Close the page cleanly and keep the site language consistent.
Layout: Simple line above the main footer, or incorporated directly into the footer.
Build note: Keep it quiet.
Final copy: Footer line: Clarity on the systems that shape how we live, lead, and grow.

5. Content model and CMS notes
• The recognition list should be editable as individual items in the CMS or page builder.
• The two context cards and three return-path cards should be editable blocks so titles, body copy, and destinations can change later without redesign.
• The page should support either a contact form, a booking link, or a mailto pattern without changing the overall layout.
• If analytics are added later, track clicks on Start a Conversation, form submissions, and clicks to Work, Inner Life, and Thoughts from the return-path section.
• Use UK spelling throughout.
6. Designer notes
• This page should feel more relational than the Work page and more practical than the About page.
• Avoid the visual language of a typical agency contact page. No oversized contact icons, no busy sidebars, and no overdesigned forms.
• Let typography and spacing carry most of the page. Use tonal shifts or thin dividers only where helpful.
• Recognition and process sections should feel trustworthy and easy to read, not like boxed feature modules.
• The context cards should visibly belong to the same site family as Work and Inner Life.
• The CTA area should feel clear and calm. The goal is to reduce friction, not create urgency.
7. Launch checklist
• Confirm 'Work Together' is active in the header nav.
• Confirm all sitemap sections are visible on the page.
• Confirm the main CTA is visible without sounding sales-heavy.
• Confirm the form or contact method is placed directly under the CTA section.
• Confirm the Work and Life context cards route to /work and /inner-life.
• Confirm the return paths route to /thoughts, /work, and /inner-life.
• Confirm the page feels consistent with Home, Work, Inner Life, Thoughts, and About.
• Confirm spacing remains generous on desktop and mobile.
