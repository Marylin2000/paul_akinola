About Page Implementation Spec
Page role
The human bridge that helps visitors understand who Paul is, why he sees things this way, and why Work and Inner Life belong in the same ecosystem.
Primary outcome
The visitor leaves with trust in the lens, a sense of the person behind it, and a clear path to keep exploring.
1. Sitemap reference and page position
This page must reflect the About branch of the sitemap clearly. Nothing should be left implied. Each sitemap section should appear as a visible or clearly represented block on the page.
Approved sitemap path for About
Opening
How You See the World
Journey
What You Care About
How You Work
Site Meaning
Next Steps
Closing Line
Footer transition
User flow for this page
Entry -> Personal connection -> Worldview -> Story -> Values -> Way of working -> How the site fits together -> Exploration
This page should feel human, grounded, reflective, and quietly confident.
It should not feel like a resume, a polished founder bio, or an inspirational manifesto.
2. Global page build rules
Header navigation: Home | Work | Inner Life | Thoughts | About | Work Together. 'About' should appear as the active item.
Tone: Personal, honest, thoughtful, and calm. No corporate bio language. No hype. No over-packaged authority claims.
Layout character: Minimal, text-led, warm whitespace, restrained visual system. This page should feel slightly more intimate than Home and more personal than Work or Thoughts.
Content width: Main text blocks should stay between 680 and 760 px max. Wider sections can expand to 1100 or 1200 px max only where needed.
Responsive behavior: Any 2-column layout should stack to one column on tablet and mobile. Reading experience should always feel calm and narrow.
Buttons and links: Keep interactions subtle. Use text links or restrained buttons only. Avoid loud CTA styling.
Visual direction: If an image is used, use one thoughtful portrait only. No collage, no logo wall, no gallery.
Page aim: Help the visitor understand who Paul is, why he sees the world this way, and why the site holds work and life together under one lens.
3. Section-by-section implementation
Section 1. Opening
Role: Establish honest presence immediately. This section should feel like meeting a real person, not reading a formal profile.
Layout: Clean opening section with narrow content width. Headline and support copy can sit beside or beneath one portrait image, but the text must remain the focus.
Build note: If a portrait is used, keep it simple and calm. Do not style this like a speaker profile or executive headshot page.
Section 2. How I See the World
Role: State the core lens behind the entire site. This is where the visitor understands the worldview that connects Work, Inner Life, and Thoughts.
Layout: Slightly wider text block or a two-column text-and-quote layout. The section should feel stable and central.
Build note: This is one of the most important parts of the page. Give it room and typographic emphasis.
Section 3. Journey
Role: Show how the lens was formed through lived experience, not theory alone.
Layout: Story-driven section with two or three short paragraphs. A minimal timeline treatment is optional only if it stays quiet.
Build note: Do not turn this into a chronological career history. Keep it reflective and selective.
Section 4. What I Care About
Role: Show the values that guide the work and the writing.
Layout: Short list, staggered statements, or a 2-column value grid. Keep it airy.
Build note: This section should feel like convictions, not slogans.
Section 5. How I Work
Role: Explain how Paul engages with people, teams, and organisations without turning the page into a service pitch.
Layout: Calm paragraph block or three short cards for Individuals, Teams, and Organisations.
Build note: This section should bridge naturally toward Work Together without sounding commercial too early.
Section 6. Site Meaning
Role: Explain why this site holds work and life together and why the Thoughts page matters.
Layout: Full-width reflective section or highlighted text band. A slightly stronger visual distinction is allowed here.
Build note: This section should make the architecture of the site feel intentional.
Section 7. Next Steps
Role: Give clear onward routes after the visitor understands the person and the lens.
Layout: Four soft navigation cards or links: Systems at Work, Systems in Life, Notes and Reflections, Work Together.
Build note: These should feel invitational, not conversion-heavy.
Section 8. Closing Line
Role: End the page with a simple human sentence that stays with the reader.
Layout: Narrow closing text block above the footer transition.
Build note: Do not over-design this. Let the line carry the weight.
Section 9. Footer transition
Role: Close the page cleanly and keep site language consistent.
Layout: Footer line above the main footer links, or integrated into the footer itself.
Build note: Footer links should remain: Home | Work | Inner Life | Thoughts | About | Work Together.
4. Content model and CMS notes
This page can be built as a static page at launch.
If the design uses a portrait image, the image should be manageable from the CMS as a single page-level asset.
Next Steps should be built as editable route cards so destinations or labels can be updated later without redesign.
If the site later introduces an explicit faith, hope, or formation taxonomy inside Thoughts, the About page should still work unchanged. It should hint at depth without depending on future categories.
The About page should not duplicate Work Together. It should build trust and orientation, then allow the visitor to choose where to go next.
If short pull-quotes or highlighted lines are used, keep them editable so phrasing can be refined later without code changes.
5. Exact copy for implementation
Section 1. Opening
Headline:
I pay attention to what most people overlook.
Support copy:
I pay attention to how things work, in systems, in people, and in myself.
This is the simplest place to understand the person behind the lens.
Section 2. How I See the World
Section heading:
How I see the world
Body copy:
Over time, I’ve come to see that most outcomes are not random.
At work, results are shaped by systems, how decisions are made, how signals move, how teams align, and how things connect beneath the surface.
In life, the same is true. Behaviour, clarity, direction, and even the way we respond under pressure are shaped by systems within us, beliefs, patterns, memory, identity, and what we carry quietly.
And underneath both, there is often more going on than what is immediately visible.
That is the lens I keep returning to.
Optional pull-quote:
Most visible problems are being shaped by something deeper.
Section 3. Journey
Section heading:
My journey
Body copy:
I did not start with this language.
I started by trying to make things work, growth, teams, decisions, outcomes, momentum. Like most people, I focused first on what I could see. The numbers. The movement. The visible friction.
But over time, I kept running into the same truth: the visible problem is rarely the real one.
That changed how I worked. I began paying closer attention to structure, signal, behaviour, and what was happening underneath the surface.
And slowly, that shift moved beyond work.
It changed how I thought about people. About growth. About clarity. About the inner life. About faith, formation, and the things that shape us long before they show up in visible outcomes.
This site is an expression of that journey. Not a finished answer. A clearer way of seeing.
Section 4. What I Care About
Section heading:
What I care about
Intro line:
A few things keep returning in how I think, write, and work.
Clarity over noise
I would rather see what is true than move quickly with the wrong assumptions.
Understanding before action
Real change usually starts with seeing what is actually shaping the problem.
Systems over surface fixes
Short-term fixes can help, but lasting progress usually depends on what sits underneath.
Growth that can hold
I care about change that is not just impressive in the moment, but sustainable over time.
Honesty about what I do not yet understand
Part of paying attention is staying teachable.
The deeper layer matters
Not everything shaping a person, a team, or a season is visible on the surface.
Section 5. How I Work
Section heading:
How I work
Body copy:
I work with individuals, teams, and organisations who are trying to make sense of what is really shaping their outcomes.
Sometimes that shows up in work, performance, growth, decisions, revenue, structure, or how teams move together.
Sometimes it shows up more personally, clarity, direction, behaviour, identity, or the tension between what someone intends and how they are actually living.
Most of the time, it is both.
I am most useful where something important is happening, but the real issue has not been named clearly enough yet.
Optional format if cards are preferred:
Individuals
Awareness, clarity, direction, and personal growth.
Teams
Shared understanding, aligned effort, and healthier ways of working together.
Organisations
Structure, signal, performance, growth, and systems that support better decisions.
Section 6. Site Meaning
Section heading:
Why this site is built this way
Body copy:
This site brings together ideas that are often kept apart.
Work and life are not separate here.
They are two ways of looking at the same deeper reality: systems shape outcomes, whether those outcomes are visible in performance, relationships, choices, growth, leadership, or the inner life.
That is why there is a Work page and an Inner Life page.
That is why Thoughts sits in the middle.
And that is why this page matters.
The goal is not to present a polished version of certainty.
The goal is to make room for clearer seeing.

6. Designer notes
This page should feel like a person, not a personal brand deck.
Keep typography calm and confident. Avoid oversized decorative treatments.
If an image is used, it should support presence, not status.
The strongest design tools here should be spacing, reading width, and hierarchy.
Values can be shown as short cards or statements, but they should not feel like corporate principles.
The Site Meaning section can carry a slightly stronger background or divider treatment to signal its importance.
The page should create trust by sounding real, not by trying to impress.
About should feel like the bridge that makes the whole site make sense.
7. Launch checklist
About is active in the header navigation.
All sitemap sections are visibly represented on the page.
Copy does not read like a resume or corporate biography.
Work, Inner Life, Thoughts, and Work Together all have clear onward routes.
Portrait image, if used, is optional and not required for the page to work.
Mobile reading width stays calm and uncluttered.
Footer language matches the rest of the site.
